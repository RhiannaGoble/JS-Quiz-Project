Result:

You are a polka dot tree frog! (Boana punctata)




One of the most common frogs of the Amazon rainforest, polka dot tree frogs secrete a biofluorescent liquid, which is thought to be used for marking their territory and communicating with potential mates. These frogs are commonly found on rafts of floating plants, travelling across the amazon basin.










Result:

You are a Western hognose snake! (Heterodon nasicus)




Despite their small size, hognoses are known for their threatening impression of a cobra. These snakes will also dramatically play dead if intimidation doesn’t go to plan, complete with a foul smell and flopped tongue. Hognoses are popular in the pet trade, despite their aggressive temperament and mild toxicity.










Result:

You are a Mexican plateau horned lizard! (Phrynosoma orbiculare)




Despite their spiky appearance, horned lizards are relatively docile, and spend most of the day waiting for insects to pass by. They confuse predators by squirting foul-tasting blood from their eyes.

Congratulations, you appear to be some kind of mammal, possibly even a human!
